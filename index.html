About the Project

As part of Saudi Arabia’s ambitious Vision 2030, the Kingdom is rapidly adopting advanced technologies like Augmented Reality (AR) to transform tourism, education, and cultural engagement. Vision 2030 is a strategic plan to diversify the economy, reduce reliance on oil, and create a vibrant society and thriving economy through innovation, sustainability, and empowerment.

Augmented Reality plays a key role in enhancing how residents and visitors interact with the Kingdom’s rich history and modern infrastructure. Through AR, tourists can experience a virtual reconstruction of ancient sites such as Al-Ula, Diriyah, and Mada'in Saleh. These overlays offer insights into how these civilizations lived, worked, and evolved, all from the palm of your hand via mobile apps or AR glasses.

In cities like Riyadh and Jeddah, AR is being incorporated into smart tourism initiatives. Visitors can follow interactive AR-guided walking tours that provide real-time information about monuments, architecture, and local traditions. Museums and galleries are increasingly using AR to bring exhibits to life, allowing guests to engage with 3D reconstructions, multilingual content, and even gamified learning experiences.

One of the most ambitious Vision 2030 projects, the NEOM megacity, is envisioned as a fully integrated smart city where AR and AI will be embedded in everyday life. Residents and tourists will use AR for real-time navigation, education, communication, and even virtual shopping experiences. AR kiosks and smart signage will offer on-the-spot digital assistance, making NEOM a true example of futuristic living.

AR is also being introduced into the Kingdom’s educational sector to help students visualize complex topics in science, history, and geography. This supports Vision 2030’s goal of modernizing education by making it more engaging, accessible, and technologically advanced.

The tourism sector is expected to contribute 10% to Saudi Arabia’s GDP by 2030. By incorporating AR into tourist experiences, the Kingdom is creating more personalized, memorable, and high-tech interactions. Whether it’s a virtual tour of a UNESCO heritage site or an immersive storytelling experience in a desert camp, AR is elevating the standards of tourism in the region.

Overall, this project aims to showcase how AR can serve as a bridge between tradition and innovation — preserving Saudi Arabia’s rich heritage while reimagining how people connect with it in the digital age. It demonstrates the Kingdom’s commitment to becoming a global leader in technology, tourism, and sustainable development.
